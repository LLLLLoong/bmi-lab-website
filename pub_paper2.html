Deep Neural Decoding of Motor Intentions using Spatiotemporal Transformers

ZHAO Liu, and ZHANG San

Abstract

Accurate interpretation of complex motor intentions from electroencephalogram (EEG) signals requires capturing both profound spatial dependencies among neural regions and long-range temporal dynamics. This study introduces the EEG-Spatiotemporal Transformer (EST), a novel self-attention mechanism specifically tailored for non-stationary biological signals. Unlike conventional CNN or RNN architectures, EST dynamically weights the channels and time frames most relevant to the executed task. We validate our model on a multi-class continuous reaching paradigm. The EST significantly outperforms traditional benchmarks, yielding an average classification accuracy of 89.2% across subjects while exhibiting exceptional robustness to electrode displacement and typical artifacts.
