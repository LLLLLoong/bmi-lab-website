Science Robotics, 2025

Sensory Feedback Restoration in Smart Prosthetics via Biomimetic Neuromodulation

CHEN Qi, SUN Ba, and ZHANG San

Abstract

The absence of somatosensory feedback represents a critical bottleneck limiting the clinical utility and psychological assimilation of bionic limbs. Here, we present a compact, non-invasive neuromodulation system capable of delivering biomimetic tactile sensations to amputees. By mapping the grip force and slip sensors of a robotic hand to engineered transcutaneous electrical nerve stimulation (TENS) parameter spaces, the system generates frequency-modulated pulse trains that mimic natural mechanoreceptor firing patterns. Clinical trials with five upper-limb amputees revealed that our biomimetic stimulation paradigm significantly reduced phantom limb pain and improved the success rate of delicate object manipulation tasks by 60%, offering a profound step towards fully closed-loop intelligent prosthetics.
